CRM server in Google sheet

Google Apps Script

  • Key highlights

  • A master Google Sheet tracks all sales leads
  • A Google Apps Script web app acts as an API endpoint, handling GET and POST requests with the master sheet
  • Sales reps pull their individual leads into their local spreadsheets
  • Changes that sales reps make locally immediately appear in the CRM sheet
  • Reps can add new leads and edit existing leads

Summary

A sales manager manages all sales leads in a Google Sheet. She needs to route leads to her team and have her reps follow up on the leads and update their status and information. Instead of having the reps update the master sheet, she uses the Google Apps Script web app to distribute leads to reps and update the master sheet based on reps' local updates.

Master sales CRM

A master Google Sheet functions as a CRM. Leads from the Marketing department are added to the sheet.

The sales manager assigns each lead to a sales rep on her team (column A). On her team, only the sales manager has access to the CRM sheet.

API endpoint

A Google Apps Script web app functions as an API server in front of the master sheet. The web app receives GET and POST requests from individual sales reps sheets. The web app enables reps to view and operate only on their leads.

A second Google Apps Script contained in the sales rep sheet uses an installable trigger to handle rep edits. The client-side script interacts with the web app. For instance, when the sales rep adds a new lead, the client-side script sends the info to the web app, which, in return, generates a new ID for the lead and sends it back to the client, which updates the sales rep sheet with the new ID.

Real-time CRUD operations

Each sales rep has his or her own sheet. The rep uses a custom menu to fetch the assigned leads from the master sheet.

The rep can update existing leads and even add new leads to their local sheet. Any such operation is immediately sent by the client script to the master Google Apps Script, which updates the master sheet.
